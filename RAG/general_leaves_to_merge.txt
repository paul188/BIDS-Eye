GENERAL-ONLY LEAVES TO FOLD INTO THEIR BROADER TERM
Audit of RAG/value_mappings.yaml  —  AUDIT ONLY, the YAML was NOT edited.
================================================================================

PURPOSE
  Mark every leaf that only represents the GENERAL category of its `broader`
  term (adds no specificity) and should therefore be dissolved INTO that broader
  group, migrating its synonyms up. This lets umbrella queries ("memory tasks",
  "perception", "field map") resolve to the group and expand to all member codes
  instead of collapsing onto a near-empty leaf.

CRITERION (strict redundancy)
  MARK leaf L iff L has a `broader` B AND L denotes the general/unspecified form
  of B's OWN concept with no new distinguishing subtype noun (only generic
  qualifiers: "general", "task", "unspecified", "various aspects of"). L ≈ B.
  EXCLUDE when L names a narrower subtype (visual/auditory/working/spatial/verbal/
  object/food/pain/walking/mandarin/optogenetic/meditation/...), or is a specific
  clinical entity even if broadly worded, or has no `broader`.

METHOD
  PyYAML pass over all sections → prefilter (name endswith `_general` OR
  label/desc says general/unspecified/NOS) = 70 candidates (63 task / 6 dx / 1
  suffix) → careful per-leaf review → reverse spot-check for catch-alls the
  prefilter missed (found 2) → verified every fold-into target exists as a group.

LEGEND   ⚠ broader-mismatch = the concept-correct group is NOT in the leaf's
         current `broader` list (folding requires re-parenting, not just merging).
